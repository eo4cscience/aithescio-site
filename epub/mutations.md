# Initial Consonant Mutations

Initial consonant mutations (Lenition and Eclipsis) are a hallmark of Celtic languages. 

## Lenition (Séimhiú)
Lenition is often triggered by certain possessive pronouns, prepositions, or in the vocative case. In Irish orthography, it is indicated by a follow-up 'h'.

## Eclipsis (Urú)
Eclipsis occurs after plural possessive pronouns and in various prepositional contexts. It involves placing a new consonant before the original one.
