# From Morphophonology to Phonotactics

The **Morphotactics** layer is the engine of word construction in our FST
pipeline. While the **Lexicon** provides the raw building blocks (stems and
tags) and the **MorphoTactics** handles the phonetic "physics," the
Morphotactic layer defines the legal combinations of these elements. It is the
grammar of the word itself.

## The Role of MorphoTactics

Morphotactics (from _morpho-_ "shape/form" and _tactics_ "arrangement")
determines how morphemes can be legally concatenated. In Irish, this involves
mapping a stem and its grammatical intent (tags) to a specific sequence of
affixes.

In our FST pipeline (`Lexicon ∘ Morphotactics`), the
Morphotactic transducer acts as the central logic gate. It takes the "Deep
String" produced by the Lexicon FST (e.g., `cloch<noun><pl>`) and transforms it
into an intermediate form with the correct suffixes attached, while also
potentially inserting technical "trigger tags" for the next layer. In the approach the morphotactics stage is sub-dived into 3 stages:

* morpho-phonology
* morphology
* phonotactics

So we can model this layers in the contexts of the whole pipeline as: 

`Lexicon ∘ (Morpho-phonology ∘ Morphology ∘ Phonotactics) ∘ Syntax`


## The State Machine of the Word

Computationally, Morphotactics is represented as a directed graph where:

- **States** represent the progress of building the word (e.g., "At Stem",
  "Adding Suffix", "Final Form").
- **Arcs** represent the legal transitions based on the input tags.

For example, if the input is a 1st Declension noun in the genitive singular, the
Morphotactic FST knows to transition through a state that triggers
palatalization (slenderization) rather than appending a standard plural suffix.

## Front Consonant Mutations

There are two kinds of front-consonant mutations in the irish language: lenition
and eclipsis. These mutations are triggered by specific grammatical contexts,
such as certain prepositions or possessive pronouns. The Morphotactic layer is
responsible for inserting the appropriate trigger tags (e.g., `<len>`, `<ecl>`)
into the intermediate representation of the word, which then signals the
Morphophonology layer to apply the correct phonetic changes.

There are many rules that govern when the lenition or eclipsis apply to a word.
Th eWikipedia page on
[Irish initial mutations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irish_initial_mutations)
provides a comprehensive overview of these rules, which are implemented in our
Morphotactic FST to ensure accurate word formation.

### lenition

The "environment" of lenition has the following rules:

- After the definite article "an" (the) in the genitive singular of feminine
  nouns and in the nominative singular of masculine nouns.
- After the possessive pronouns "_mo_" (my), "_do_" (your), and "_a_"
  (his/her/its) when they refer to a noun that is not a proper noun.
- After certain prepositions, such as "_le_" (with), "_do_" (to), and "_ar_"
  (on).
- In the vocative case, which is used when directly addressing someone or
  something.
- After certain prepositions that trigger lenition, such as "_i_" (in), "_ar_"
  (on), and "_le_" (with).
- After the preterite/conditional of the copula "is" (is/was/will be), which is
  "ba" in the preterite and "bheadh" in the conditional.
- After certain preverbal particles, such as "_ní_" (not), "_an_" (interrogative
  particle), and "_go_" (subjunctive particle).
- A verb in the preterite, imperfect or conditional tense is lenited when it
  follows the conjunction "_go_" (that) in a subordinate clause.
- After certain numbers, _aon bhó amháin_ "one cow", _an chéad bhliain_ "the
  first year", _dhá theach_ "two houses"
- After preposed adjectivesc onstructions of adjective + noun that are written
  as compounds. For example, "_sean-bhean_" "old woman", "_deacair-chara_"
  "difficult friend", "_fada-bhuí_".
- After most prefixes like _an-bheag_ "very small", _róbheag_ "too small"
  _aisghabháil_ "retake", and _athbhliain_ "new year".
- The second part of a compound like: _ainmfhocal_ "noun" (lit. "name word"
  _dúghorm_ "dark blue", _státfhiach_ "national debt".
- In head + modifier constructions their constructions coronals are lenited even
  following other: _aimsir bháistí_ "rainy weather" (lenition after a feminine
  singular noun), _buidéil shú_ "bottles of juice" (lenition after a plural
  ending in a slender consonant), _ teach Sheáin_ "Seán's house" (lenition of a
  definite noun in the genitive)
- Postposed adjectives in certain circumstances such as:_bean dheas_ "a pretty
  woman" (lenition after a feminine singular noun) _na fir mhóra_ "the big men"
  (lenition after a plural noun ending in a slender consonant) _ainm an fhir
  bhig_ "the name of the small man" (lenition after a masculine singular noun in
  the genitive)

### eclipsis

The environment for eclipsis is provided in the following list.

- After plural possessive pronouns. The possessive pronouns that trigger
  eclipsis are _ár_ "our", _bhur_ "your (pl.)", _a_ "their" _ár gcairde "our
  friends" _bhur bpáistí "your (pl.) children", _a mbád "their boat",
- After certain numbers. The numbers that trigger eclipsis (the noun being in
  the singular) are: _seacht gcapall_ "seven horses", _ocht n-asal_ "eight
  donkeys", _naoi gcat_ "nine cats", _deich bpeann_ "ten pens", _ocht n-asal_
  "eight donkeys", _naoi gcat_ "nine cats", _deich bpeann_ "ten pens"
- After certain prepositions that trigger eclipsis, such as _i_ "in", _ar_ "on",
  and _le_ "with". For example, _i gcarcair_ "in prison", _ar gcarr_ "on a car",
  _le gcapall_ "with a horse".
- Genitive plural nouns after the definite article. The genitive plural article
  na eclipses a following noun: _na n-asal_ "of the donkeys", _na bhfocal_ "of
  the words", _na bhfocal_ "of the words"
- Dative singular nouns after the definite article for certain western and
  southern dialects: _ag an bhfear_ "by the man" _ar an gcrann_ "on the tree".
- After certain preverbal particles such as: _an poll a dtagann na coiníní as_
  "the hole that the rabbits come out of", _An dtagann sé gach lá_? "Does he
  come every day?", _Cá bhfuil mo spéaclaí_? "Where are my glasses?", _Dúirt sé
  go dtiocfadh sé_. "He said that he would come.", _dá mbeadh a fhios sin agam_
  "if I had known that".

## Separation into Paradigms: an t-ábh

Because the rules for different word classes in Irish are so distinct, we
implement the Morphotactic layer as a collection of specialized transducers,
which are then unioned together:

1.  **Nominal Paradigm:** Handles the 5 declensions of nouns and adjective
    agreement.
2.  **Verbal Paradigm:** Manages the complex systems of tense, aspect, and mood.
3.  **Closed Functional Forms:** Covers prepositional pronouns, articles, and
    numerals.

By isolating these rules, we ensure that a change to the way verbs are
conjugated does not inadvertently affect how nouns are declined, maintaining the
modularity and stability of the entire system.


## The Full Connection: From Lexicon to Surface

To illustrate this clearly, let's use the Irish feminine noun bróg (shoe). We will put it into the genitive case (meaning "of the shoe," as in "the color of the shoe"), which requires both a morphological suffix and a phonotactic adjustment.

### The Pipeline for An Bhróige (Of the shoe)

**Input (Lexical Stage)**:The system starts with the root and the grammar tags:Art + _bróg_ + Noun + Fem + Sing + Gen

**Morphophonology (Early Stage)**:The grammar rules for the feminine genitive singular with the article an trigger an initial mutation (Lenition).Rule: b → bh Result: _An bhróg_...

**Morphology (Suffixation)**:The genitive case for this class of nouns requires the addition of the suffix -e.Intermediate Form: An bhróg + e → _An bhróge*_ (This is "illegal" in Irish).

**Phonotactics (Late Stage - The "Double Change")**:The phonotactic rule Caol le caol (Slender with slender) now acts as a mandatory filter. Because the suffix -e is a slender vowel, the preceding consonant g must also become slender (palatalized) to be "legal".
* Constraint: A broad consonant (velarized) cannot immediately precede a slender vowel.
* Adjustment: To show the g is now slender, a slender vowel (i) must be inserted before it to "bridge" the sound.
* Surface Form: _An bhróige_
