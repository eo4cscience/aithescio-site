# Project Introduction: A Modern Morphological Parser for Irish

Modern Irish is a Goidelic Celtic language characterized by complex fusional morphology, initial consonant mutations (lenition and eclipsis), and significant stem allomorphy. Because a single lemma can appear in dozens of different surface forms depending on its grammatical context, a robust morphological parser is critical for almost any downstream Natural Language Processing (NLP) task. 

We are building a comprehensive morphological parser for Modern Irish. Our project is designed to address several critical modern use cases:

*   **Machine Translation (MT) Pre-processing & Post-processing:** Neural Machine Translation (NMT) models struggle with highly inflected languages due to "data sparsity" (too many rare word forms). Our parser lemmatizes Irish text before it feeds into an LLM or NMT model, normalizing the input. Conversely, it generates correctly inflected and mutated Irish surface forms from abstract representations output by downstream models.
*   **Search and Information Retrieval (IR):** When searching for a lemma like *"bád"* (boat), our parser enables systems to instantly recognize and return documents containing *"bháid"* (of a boat), *"mbáid"* (their boat), *"báid"* (boats), and other morphologically expanded forms.
*   **Spell Checking and Grammar Correction:** Given the strict rules governing mutations in Irish (e.g., *mo mhadra* vs. *ár madra*), our tool identifies morphotactic violations and suggests grammatically correct forms, making it an invaluable resource for Computer-Assisted Language Learning (CALL).
*   **Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) and Text-to-Speech (TTS):** We provide the morphological blueprints necessary for Grapheme-to-Phoneme (G2P) conversion, ensuring that the drastic pronunciation changes caused by grammatical mutations are accurately mapped.
*   **Corpus Annotation and Lexicography:** Our parser automatically annotates massive corpora with Part-of-Speech (POS) tags, lemma information, and morphological features, saving thousands of hours of manual linguistic labor.

## The Value Add of Our Pynini-Based Approach

While Finite State Transducers (FSTs) have been the gold standard for Irish morphology for decades—with pioneering institutions like DCU and Trinity College Dublin utilizing tools like Xerox Finite-State Tools (XFST) and `foma`—our project introduces massive, game-changing value additions by building a **modern architecture utilizing Pynini**:

### A. Seamless Python Ecosystem Integration
Older FST tools rely on domain-specific languages and standalone compiled binaries, making them difficult to integrate into modern NLP pipelines. Pynini (a Python wrapper around Google's OpenFst library) allows us to define, compile, and execute FSTs directly within a Python environment. This means our parser seamlessly interfaces with modern NLP libraries like **spaCy, HuggingFace Transformers, PyTorch, and NLTK**.

### B. Enabling Neuro-Symbolic AI Architectures
The bleeding edge of NLP involves combining the rigid, rule-based accuracy of FSTs with the probabilistic power of Large Language Models (LLMs). Because we use a Python-native approach, we can easily build a "Neuro-Symbolic" architecture. An LLM predicts the semantic intent and morphological tags, and our Pynini FST acts as a strict deterministic generator, ensuring the final generated Irish text obeys strict mutation rules perfectly—something LLMs frequently get wrong.

### C. Weighted Finite-State Transducers (WFSTs)
Unlike basic FSTs, our Pynini foundation natively supports *weighted* operations over semirings. If an Irish word form is highly ambiguous and could parse to three different lemmas, we apply statistical weights (derived from corpus frequencies) to the paths in our transducer, returning the *most probable* morphological analysis seamlessly.

### D. Maintenance, Expressivity, and Open Source Longevity
Writing morphological rules in raw OpenFst C++ is tedious, and legacy XFST is an increasingly niche skill. Our approach allows us to write highly expressive, clean Python string-compilation rules. We get the incredible execution speed of OpenFst combined with the readable syntax and maintainability of Python, significantly lowering the barrier to entry for new developers.

***

**Summary:** 
By porting established Celtic linguistic logic to a **Pynini-backed architecture**, we are elevating this Irish morphological parser from a standalone linguistic experiment into a highly-scalable, production-ready software component, bridging the gap between traditional linguistics and the modern Deep Learning ecosystem.
