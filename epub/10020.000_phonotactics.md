# Phonotactics: <br><span style="font-size: 0.5em;">cleaning to make the final surface form of the word</span>

The **Phonotactics** layer is the last stage in the morphotactics conversion. It takes the output of the morphology layer and assembles the final surface form of the word. In the case of the Irish language this involves removing any epenthetic vowels that were added during the morphology stage and ensuring that the final surface form of the word is a valid Irish word or forcing '_Caol le Caol agus Leathan le Leathan_' to ensure the correct context of consonants.
